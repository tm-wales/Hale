Green and Eco friendly Construction

1.     Eco friendly Construction

Sustainably is a core value which is reflected in how Hale design their energy efficient homes and how the business is run.  All houses are designed to be energy efficiency and use sustainable materials wherever possible.  The timber frame houses are produced using timber which is responsible forest and managed.  Cutting edge insulation techniques, using a foam insulation product which can cuts energy bills by up to 90%. 

Biomass boilers have been installed at the Neath head office and are fuelled by offcuts from its sister company Sevenoaks Modular. Not only does this ensure that resources are not wasted, it also saves on the company’s energy consumption.

 

Green Living

Hale have a passion for nature and the environment which is stemmed from Jonathan and his family.  With every development, the environmental impact is considered and all aspects are taken to reduce these effects.  Many of their developments encourage wild life by planting trees, hedgerows and bee –friendly plants and erecting bird and bat boxes.   Sites will often include open spaces and green areas because of the positive impact on both nature and the community.

Health and Safety

Hale believe health and safety is the most important consideration before any construction project is initiated. The construction industry is prone to hazardous situations and can be dangerous a lot of the time. Hale are very proactive in accident prevention and provide personnel, weekly training updates on site and relevant training throughout the year to ensure they are fully aware of all health and safety aspects and promotes a positive attitude to H & S. As well as offering employees suitable training and information, Hale regular complete risk assessments and take any necessary steps to reduce the dangers identified in these analyses.

Key Facts

  • Health and safety failures cost Great Britain up to £18 billion each year.
  • 400 people every year are still killed in accidents caused by work activities.
  • Around 25 million working days are lost every year as a result of work-related accidents and ill-health.
  • Over 25,000 people are forced to give up work every year as a result of work-related accidents and ill-health.
  • Around 2 million people - 5 per cent of the population - suffer from ill-health caused by work.
